Investigating a Dataset¶

TMDb Movies¶

Today we will be analyzing the TMDb data set from Kaggle. This data set has almost 11,000 movie entries that include the movie's title, budget, revenue, ratings and many other columns with detialed information. We will assess the data, clean, format it and analyze it.

Questions to answer:

  1. What are the most popular genre in the dataframe?
  2. Do films with higher voting counts have a higher profit?;
  3. What is the average runtime?
  4. Is runtime associated with higher profits?;
  5. Which movies generated the most/least profit?;

Conclusions¶

Analysing this data set was very instructive. I was able to apply the whole data analysis process to the data set. Seeing how we take a table full of names and numbers and transform it to someting everyone can digest produces a very rewarding feeling. The data wrangling process was very challenging for me. However, the most difficult step is to make visualizations that convey a story.

Answers to questions:

  1. What are the most popular genre in the dataframe? -The predominant genres are "drama", "comedy", "thiller" and "action". Some of the other genres might be subcategories of these as well.

  2. Do films with higher voting counts have a higher profit? -The correlation is not super strong, but it is sufficient for us to see something here. Of course, we are not infering that more vote "cause" higher profits. However, this might be the case. The outliers in this here should still be considered. It makes sense for the most successful movies to have both the highest voting counts and profits.

  3. What is the average runtime? -Our average film is one hour and 49 minutes long. However, we need to consider that there were many "short" films included here.
  4. Is runtime associated with higher profits? -It seems like movies with runtime of 120 mins or higher average higher profits. This doesn't mean there is a direct correlation between the two.However, The "profits" average is a negative number only once in the dataframe (especially for runtimes > 120 mins), for an outlier.
  5. Which movies generated the most/least profit? -"Avatar" is the profit winner. This movie holds the record for the highest grossing film in history! "The Warrior's Way" is the losser tonight, generated almost $414 million in losses!

Limitations

Our analysis was very well hindered by some limitations. Some of these were within our reach to overcome (e.g., by doing external research), others were completely outside of our control and the scope of this project.

  • One limitation we encountered was the number of missing and null values we had in the original dataset. Some of these were insignificant like "cast" and "homepage", others were more serious like have a movie's runtime be "0". There were also "0" values for important metrics like budget and revenue.
  • Another limitation was that the dataset was packed with outliers, which we delath with to an extent, but still couldn't get completely rid of them. How can we determine who's an outlier or not in this dataset. This queston could be answer, but lies outside of the scope of this analysis.
  • Another limitation was encountered when analyzing the genres. Most movies fall inside of multiple genre categories. Other genres could be considered subcategorical as well (i.e., "dramatic thillers", "action crime", "dramatic comedies", etc...)
